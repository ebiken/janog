= Optical Transceiver & Cable Quick Reference
:toc:
:toclevels: 2

Quick reference for optical transceiver and cable types used in Ethernet networks, covering 10G through 1.6T.

Abbreviations: MMF = Multimode Fiber, SMF = Single-Mode Fiber, CWDM = Coarse WDM,
LWDM = Long-reach WDM, PAM4 = Pulse-Amplitude Modulation 4-level, NRZ = Non-Return-to-Zero,
DAC = Direct-Attach Copper, AOC = Active Optical Cable, APC = Angled Physical Contact.

== Breakout Reference

Breakout cables allow a single high-speed port to connect to multiple lower-speed ports.
Passive fanout cables split parallel fiber physically. WDM demux modules split wavelengths optically.
Active breakout cables include electronics for lane retiming.

[cols="3,3,3,3",options="header"]
|===
| Source Port | Target Port(s) | Cable / Method | Notes

| 800GBASE-DR8
| 2x 400GBASE-DR4
| MPO-16 to 2x MPO-12 APC passive fanout
| 8 parallel SMF -> split to two 4-lane groups

| 800GBASE-DR8
| 8x 100GBASE-DR
| MPO-16 to 8x LC Duplex passive fanout
| 8 parallel SMF -> split to 8 individual fibers

| 800GBASE-FR8
| 2x 400GBASE-FR4
| CWDM demux module
| 8 wavelengths -> two 4-lambda groups

| 800GBASE-LR8
| 2x 400GBASE-LR4
| LWDM demux module
| 8 wavelengths -> two 4-lambda groups

| 800G QSFP-DD800
| 2x 400G QSFP-DD
| Active breakout DAC/AOC
| Electronic lane retiming; 1-3m DAC or 10-30m AOC

| 400GBASE-DR4
| 4x 100GBASE-DR
| MPO-12 APC to 4x LC Duplex passive fanout
| 4 parallel SMF -> 4 individual fiber pairs

| 400GBASE-FR4
| 4x 100GBASE-FR
| CWDM demux module
| 4 CWDM wavelengths -> 4 individual ports

| 400GBASE-LR4
| 4x 100GBASE-LR
| LWDM demux module
| 4 LWDM wavelengths -> 4 individual ports

| 400GBASE-SR8
| 2x 200GBASE-SR4
| MPO-16 to 2x MPO-12 passive fanout
| 8-lane MMF -> two 4-lane groups

| 400GBASE-SR8
| 8x 50GBASE-SR
| MPO-16 to 8x LC Duplex passive fanout
| 8-lane MMF -> 8 individual LC Duplex pairs

| 400G QSFP-DD (DAC)

| QSFP-DD to 4x QSFP28 active breakout DAC/AOC
| 2 electrical lanes per QSFP28 target

| 400G QSFP-DD (DAC)

| QSFP-DD to 8x SFP56 active breakout DAC/AOC
| 1 electrical lane per SFP56 target

| 200GBASE-DR4

| 4 parallel SMF -> 4 individual fiber pairs

| 200GBASE-SR4
| 4x 50GBASE-SR
| MPO-12 to 4x LC Duplex passive fanout
| 4-lane MMF -> 4 individual LC Duplex pairs

| 100GBASE-SR4

| MPO-12 to 4x LC Duplex passive fanout

== Connectors

[cols="2,1,3,4",options="header"]
|===
| Connector | Fibers | Common Applications | Notes

| LC Duplex
| 2 (Tx+Rx)
| SFP+, SFP28, SFP56; QSFP28/56/DD LR/ER/ZR; CFP family
| Most common; low insertion loss (0.3 dB typical)

| SC Duplex
| 2 (Tx+Rx)
| Legacy telecom equipment
| Larger than LC; mostly legacy deployments

| MPO-8
| 8
| Legacy 40G parallel
| 4 Tx + 4 Rx; superseded by MPO-12

| MPO-12
| 12
| 40G-SR4, 100G-SR4, 100G-DR (PSM4), 200G-SR4, 400G-DR4
| SR4/DR4: 4 Tx + 4 Rx + 4 unused; PSM4: 4 Tx + 4 Rx + 4 unused

| MPO-16
| 16
| 400G-SR8, 800G-SR8, 800G-DR8
| 8 Tx + 8 Rx; full lane utilization

| MPO-24
| 24
| Emerging 1.6T applications
| Future high-density parallel fiber

|===

MPO connectors used with SMF parallel applications (DR variants) require APC (angled physical contact)
polish to reduce back-reflection. MMF SR applications use UPC (ultra physical contact) polish.
MTP is a registered trademark for enhanced MPO connectors (compatible with standard MPO).

== Coherent optics (industry)

These are commonly deployed in Ethernet networks but are specified by industry agreements (e.g., OIF / MSAs),
not IEEE 802.3 PMD names like `400GBASE-*`.

[cols="3,2,3,2,2",options="header"]

| 400ZR (OIF)

| SMF
| C-band DWDM tunable
| LC Duplex

| 400ZR+ / OpenZR+ (MSA)
| Vendor-specific (often >120 km)
| SMF
| C-band DWDM tunable
| LC Duplex

|===
